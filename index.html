About Myanmar

Discover the enchanting blend of natural beauty, ancient heritage and warm hospitality

The Land of Golden Pagodas

Myanmar, formerly known as Burma, is a mystical land located in Southeast Asia. Bordered by China, Thailand, India, Laos and Bangladesh, it's a country blessed with rich biodiversity, abundant natural resources, and a cultural heritage dating back thousands of years.

After decades of isolation, Myanmar has opened its doors to the world, revealing its untouched beauty and preserved traditions. From the shimmering spires of Shwedagon Pagoda to the ancient temples of Bagan, the natural beauty of Inle Lake, and the pristine beaches of Ngapali, Myanmar offers experiences that captivate the heart and inspire the soul.

Geography

Myanmar spans over 676,578 sq km, featuring diverse landscapes from mountains and highlands to plains, valleys, and over 2,000 km of coastline along the Bay of Bengal and Andaman Sea.

Population

Home to over 54 million people from 135 distinct ethnic groups, each with their unique customs, languages, and traditions, creating a rich cultural mosaic.

History

With a history spanning over 2,000 years, Myanmar has witnessed powerful kingdoms, British colonization, independence in 1948, and decades of military rule before recent democratic reforms.

Culture & Traditions

Immerse yourself in Myanmar's rich cultural heritage and ancient traditions

Buddhism & Spirituality

Buddhism is deeply woven into the fabric of Myanmar's society, influencing daily life, art, and architecture. Monks in their distinctive maroon robes are a common sight, and pagodas dot the landscape as symbols of devotion.

Thanaka

The distinctive yellow-white paste made from ground bark is a unique aspect of Myanmar culture. Applied to the face as both cosmetic and sunscreen, it's a traditional beauty practice dating back centuries.

Traditional Dress

The longyi, a sarong-like garment worn by both men and women, is the traditional attire of Myanmar. Each ethnic group has its own distinctive patterns and styles, reflecting their unique cultural identity.

Culinary Delights

Explore the rich and diverse flavors of Myanmar's traditional cuisine

Mohinga

Considered Myanmar's national dish, Mohinga is a fragrant fish soup with rice noodles, typically enjoyed for breakfast. It's seasoned with lemongrass, ginger, and served with crispy fritters, boiled eggs, and fresh herbs.

Lahpet Thoke

A unique fermented tea leaf salad that perfectly balances sour, spicy, and savory flavors. Mixed with crunchy beans, nuts, sesame seeds, and seasoned with lime juice and garlic oil.

Shan Noodles

Originating from the Shan State, this beloved noodle dish features rice noodles in a flavorful chicken or pork broth, topped with marinated meat, crunchy peanuts, and chili oil.

Mont Lin Ma Yar

Known as "couple snacks," these are small rice pancakes with quail eggs, spring onions, and other toppings. The batter is cooked in a special pan with rounded molds.

Ohn No Khao Swe

A coconut milk-based chicken curry served with egg noodles, garnished with crispy fried onions, chili oil, and hard-boiled eggs. A creamy, comforting dish.

Nan Gyi Thoke

A hearty salad made with thick rice noodles, chicken curry, chickpea flour, and chili oil. It's typically garnished with sliced onions, lime, and crispy fried fritters.

Htamin Jin

A rice salad from the Shan State made with fermented rice, tomatoes, garlic oil, and turmeric. It has a tangy flavor and is usually served with deep-fried pork rinds.

Burmese Curry

Unlike the curries of neighboring countries, Burmese curries are less spicy and more savory, with a rich oil-based sauce. They come in various proteins including chicken, pork, fish, or vegetable options.
